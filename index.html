Section 01  •  Why Aditi

The Largest Untapped Differentiator
in the CAT Portfolio

An operator who knows how to run a CAT machine should feel that distinctiveness the moment they touch the controls. Today that experience is inconsistent, siloed, and undocumented at the strategy level.

⚙
Physical HMI Fluency

The UX Strategy Lead and Senior Researcher are evaluated on physical HMI background in industrial environments. Joystick ergonomics, in-cab spatial conventions, and control layout under vibration and noise are a different discipline from digital product strategy. Candidates without direct equipment operator design experience are not considered.

📍
Embedded Full-Time Presence

The Senior Researcher is on-site in Peoria, IL full-time for the full engagement. Full-time embedded presence builds institutional trust with CAT's team that cannot be replicated through scheduled visits. Contextual inquiry with operators in working conditions cannot be conducted remotely or in compressed site visits.

🤝
Co-Led Research

CAT component leads participate in developing the findings rather than only reviewing them at the end. Teams who co-created the evidence are stakeholders in the outcome. A single named senior practitioner from Aditi AI Labs serves as both Principal AI Architect and Senior Advisor to the engagement.

🔩
Proven Industrial AI Stack — Live in Production

Aditi's AI Labs team recently delivered a custom HMI design system and operator interface platform for a leading rail technology OEM, built for industrial equipment operators under degraded connectivity, with MCP server integration and Figma Maker automation live in production. The same team and delivery stack are committed to CAT Phase 1.

Section 02  •  Approach

Two Sequential Workstreams,
One Strategic Foundation

Discovery must be substantially complete before UX Strategy synthesis begins. The transition is marked by a formal Discovery Readout that CAT stakeholders review and approve.

What Discovery Includes
Stakeholder InterviewsStructured interviews with CAT leadership, product owners, engineering leads, and field SMEs. Custom Discussion Guide aligned to strategic priorities. Synthesized into a Stakeholder Interview Summary Report covering alignment gaps, success definitions, and strategic tensions.
HMI Experience AuditStructured audit of existing operator interface assets — component libraries, design documentation, in-cab display patterns, and physical control configurations across selected product lines. Output: a gap inventory organized by severity.
Field & Operator ResearchOn-site contextual inquiry conducted jointly with CAT's internal UX research team. Aditi's Senior Researcher is embedded full-time. Sessions document workflow breakdowns, cognitive load patterns, physical interaction habits, and unmet needs that do not surface in lab or interview settings.
What Discovery Produces
Evidence-Based Current State PictureA clear picture of the current operator experience: gaps, friction points, moments that matter most, and constraints (offline operation, mixed fleet, regulatory environment) that any future design must accommodate.
Validated Research RecordRaw session notes, structured observation reports, and supporting materials — organized for direct input into UX Strategy synthesis.
▶  Discovery Gate — Required Before UX Strategy Begins

Aditi presents consolidated Discovery findings in a structured readout session. Both teams align on what the research shows, surface and resolve conflicting interpretations, and formally confirm the priority operator experience problems the UX Strategy must address. CAT's written approval is required before UX Strategy begins.

UX Strategy Document Contains
Operator Archetypes (3–5)Research-grounded behavioral and cognitive profiles of the distinct operator types CAT equipment serves. Each archetype documents primary tasks, physical interaction patterns, environmental context, tolerance for error, and relationship to technology.
Design Principles (5–7)Governing principles derived from research — each stated as a directive ("Reduce operator cognitive load at peak decision moments"), illustrated with a concrete example, and accompanied by a counter-example of what it explicitly rules out.
HMI Experience VisionWritten and conceptual articulation of the target operator experience. Not a prototype — a clear description grounded in operator archetypes and expressed through 2–3 representative operator scenarios (e.g., dozer operator transitioning from GPS-guided grading to manual finish pass).
End-to-End Experience MapMaps seven stages — Discovery, Purchase, Deploy, Activate, Training, Operate, Maintenance — with HMI touchpoints. Strategic insight: most HMI work over-focuses on Operate, while perception of the machine is set in the first hours of use.
AI Opportunity MapStructured assessment of where AI integration creates genuine operator value versus where it adds complexity without benefit. Evaluated against four criteria: operator context fit, connectivity constraints, safety classification, and Phase 2 implementation feasibility.
Strategic RecommendationsPrioritized Phase 2 investment recommendations organized by operator impact and implementation complexity. Directly feeds D9 (Phase 2 Readiness Roadmap) and D8 (Business Value Assessment).
Use Cases & Design WorkstreamDefinition of the design sprint philosophy; establishment of repeatable cycle framework: discover, build, prototype, test. Includes sprint development progression: Assumption validation, MVP definition, Experience validation, Expansion and refinement.
What the UX Strategy Is NOT

The UX Strategy document does not contain wireframes, screen designs, component specifications, or interaction prototypes. Those are Phase 2 outputs. The strategy defines what to build and why; Phase 2 defines how to build it.

The following items are explicitly excluded from Phase 1 scope. Each is either handled by a separate CAT team, governed by a separate Statement of Work, or deferred to Phase 2 or Phase 3.

  • Design or development of new HMI components or display interfaces
  • In-depth digital product UX strategy (handled by separate CAT Digital team)
  • Physical component fabrication or manufacturing specifications
  • Independent operator interviews conducted without CAT researcher accompaniment
  • Embedded display screen content design (handled separately by CAT's existing digital UX team)
  • Detailed Phase 2 or Phase 3 scoping (each governed by separate Statements of Work)
Section 03  •  Deliverables

Ten Deliverables.
Two Workstreams. One Gate.

Discovery (D1–D5) builds the evidence base. UX Strategy (D6–D10) synthesizes it into a durable strategic framework. CAT's written approval of the Discovery Readout is required before UX Strategy begins.

D1
Stakeholder Interview Plan & Discussion Guide
Structured plan and facilitation guide for M2 interviews. Identifies participants by role and business unit, defines interview sequencing, and contains the full question set with branching logic for different stakeholder profiles. CAT's UX Manager reviews and approves before interviews begin.
M1 — Wks 1–2
D2
Stakeholder Interview Summary Report
Synthesis document capturing findings across all stakeholder interviews, organized by theme to surface areas of alignment, active disagreement, and gaps between leadership expectations and business positioning. Each theme supported by direct quotes attributed to role type. Explicit section on conflicting success definitions.
M2 — Wks 2–7
D3
Current State Assessment: HMI Experience Audit
Structured audit of existing operator interface assets: physical controls, in-cab display content and behavior, indicator and warning systems, and any existing design documentation or component libraries. Output is a gap inventory organized by severity, with highest-risk experience failures prioritized for direct address in the UX Strategy.
M3 — Wks 4–9
D4
User Research Coordination Framework
Planning and coordination document produced before field research begins, defining the methodology, schedule, and roles for joint Aditi–CAT operator research sessions. Documents any constraints or access limitations identified during planning that may affect research scope.
M4 — Wks 8–13
D5
Field & Operator Research Observations Report
Primary research output of the Discovery workstream. Organized around operator tasks and decision moments — capturing how operators actually behave in field conditions, not how procedures say they should. Raw session notes and supporting materials included as an appendix.
M4 — Wks 8–13
▶  Discovery Readout Gate
Before UX Strategy synthesis begins, Aditi presents consolidated Discovery findings in a structured readout session with CAT's UX Manager and designated stakeholders. Both teams align on findings, resolve conflicting interpretations, and formally confirm the priority operator experience problems the UX Strategy must address. CAT's written approval is required before UX Strategy begins.
M4 Gate
D6
AI Opportunity Assessment
Structured evaluation of where AI integration creates genuine, measurable operator value — and where it introduces complexity without benefit. Opportunities evaluated against: operator context fit, offline feasibility (can this function offline?), safety classification (does AI involvement require regulatory review?), and Phase 2 implementation feasibility. Led by the Principal AI Architect from Aditi AI Labs.
M5 — Wks 11–15
D7
Operator Experience UX Strategy Document
The primary strategic deliverable of Phase 1 and the direct input to Phase 2 scoping. Synthesizes all Discovery findings into a durable framework defining what the CAT operator experience should be, why, and how to build toward it. Contains: operator archetypes, design principles, HMI experience vision, end-to-end experience map, AI opportunity integration, and strategic recommendations. Subject to 2–3 iterative review cycles with CAT's UX Manager.
M6 — Wks 14–21
D8
Business Value Assessment
Structured analysis quantifying the business value of investing in a unified CAT operator experience. Translates UX Strategy findings into Phase 2 investment decision terms: productivity impact, safety, fleet lifecycle, and competitive differentiation. Value estimates supported by proxy data from comparable industrial HMI programs with assumptions stated explicitly.
M7 — Wks 20–24
D9
Phase 2 Readiness Roadmap & Recommendations
The document that scopes Phase 2. Translates the UX Strategy, AI Opportunity Map, and Business Value Assessment into a concrete Phase 2 recommendation: proposed scope and deliverable structure, team composition, timeline framework, investment range, identified dependencies and risks, and recommended sequencing of work within Phase 2.
M7 — Wks 20–24
D10
Phase 1 Final Presentation
Executive-level presentation synthesizing Phase 1 findings, the UX Strategy, business value case, and Phase 2 recommendation for delivery to CAT leadership. Designed for an audience that has not been involved in the day-to-day work of Phase 1 — telling the complete story from problem framing through strategic recommendation in a format suitable for executive review and Phase 2 go/no-go decision-making. Accompanied by a knowledge transfer package containing all Phase 1 deliverables in organized, accessible form.
M8 — Wks 25–26

The Engagement — Rail Technology OEM

Aditi designed and built a next-generation HMI platform for a leading rail technology OEM — delivering operator interfaces, control patterns, and a custom HMI design system for a fleet of autonomous rail inspection robots operating across active rail environments.

The platform supports real-time operator decision-making under degraded connectivity and across mixed equipment, requiring sub-second latency, offline-first field access, and a modular design system scalable across independently deployed inspection modules.

Custom HMI Design System built over the client's existing component library, extended with rail-specific operator controls, state patterns, and interaction conventions. Angular 20+ microfrontend platform with Native Federation. Progressive Web App architecture for full platform access from field devices without connectivity dependency.

Real-Time Fleet Ops

Multiple autonomous sleds simultaneously with sub-second latency across degraded rail environments.

Offline-First Access

Full platform access from field devices without connectivity dependency — live in production.

MCP + Figma Maker

MCP server integration and Figma Maker AI-assisted component generation — live in production.

7 Inspection Modules

Independently deployed with isolated build pipelines; new types added without platform disruption.

Direct Relevance to CAT
Industrial Operator Context

Same core challenge: operators using equipment in field conditions, not digital product consumers. HMI built for the human operating the machine, not the engineer reviewing the spec.

Full platform access without connectivity dependency. Directly applicable to CAT's underground mining, remote sites, and GPS-denied operation scenarios.

Custom HMI Design System

Built from the ground up for industrial equipment — not adapted from a digital product system. Same methodology Aditi will apply to CAT Phase 1 and Phase 2.

MCP Design Automation Live
